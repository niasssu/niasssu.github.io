Black Friday Deals

you sometimes see deals that are "buy a get b free" or something, which is the same as "pay for a, get (a+b)". this can be generalized further to "pay for x, get y", resulting in a ratio of x:y ; x being how much you pay for, and y how much you actually get.

i get why businesses want to do "buy a get b free" sales, since they have a lot of inventory they want/need to get rid of, but a lot of people don't want to buy that many of the same thing! and there are some deals that just aren't that good, or are the exact opposite of deals.

an immediate example i can think of as the "opposite of a deal" (a scam?) is dollar tree's 25 cent markup. basically there was this thing that happened in the states that required all stores to increase prices by 25 cents at minimum. trying to stay as close to a dollar as they could, dollar tree did the bare minimum and increased from 1 to 1.25. but now this means that when you buy four things, you're paying for five: "pay for 5, get 4". of course, it was simply them doing the bare minimum to comply with the legal rules, but without knowing that, you would've thought it was an intentioanl scam.

"buy a get b free" sales aren't that effective most of the time anyway, most of the time people want to see a fraction or percentage. people who can see through bagb sales realize most of them just aren't worth it. b1g1 is the same as 1/2 off, b2g1 is 1/3 off. so on.

this was abt black friday deals wasn't it? yeah, they're generally just as bad now, it's effectively the same as "holiday deals" these days. why is black friday suddenly a holy day? because americans love the illusion of saving money, and apparently created a deity for it. couldn't tell you what deity that is, i'm not in-touch enough to know.
